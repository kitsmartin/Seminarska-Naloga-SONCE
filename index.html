Wynand Otto Jan Nieuwenkamp



Introduction

"W.O.J. Nieuwenkamp" was a Dutch graphic, artist, painter, architect, boat builder, writer, explorer, art collector and ethnographer.



Early Life

Wijnand Otto Jan Nieuwenkamp was born on 27 July 1874 in Amsterdam. Nieuwenkamp started drawing when he was a child and decided that he wanted to be an artist. His father disagreed with his decision and sent him to grammar school in Haarlem. But it wasn’t long before Nieuwenkamp failed and decided to continue his education in Amsterdam. He took some lessons at the Amsterdamse Kunstnijverheidsschool (Amsterdam School of Applied Arts) and he graduated in 1892. He started working in his father's office, but it wasn't a big success. In 1894 Nieuwenkamp studied at the National School of Crafts in Amsterdam but it wasn’t long before he left and decided to learn about the profession of art on his own.




Beginning of his activity

From that time onwards Nieuwenkamp became self-sufficient, creating illustrations for various magazines. He established his own studio in Haarlem where he met other famous Dutch artist who influenced his style. His works show influences of Jugendstil or Art Nouveau.



Trips to Bali

From 1898 onwards, numerous journeys took him to the islands of the East Indian archipelago, especially to Bali. He was cycling around and using his drawing tools to quickly capture all his impressions. It were those journeys to the Netherlands East Indies that had a particularly strong influence on his development as an artist.



De Zwerver

In 1900 Nieuwenkamp married Anna Wilbrink from Lunteren, a distant cousin. For the first few years they lived on the De Zwerver, a beautiful houseboat which had its own studio, a houseboat that he had designed himself.



Last Years

In 1910 the family settled down in Edam. By the end of the 1920s he left the Netherlands, moved first to Sicily and then to Rome. In 1926 they settled in San Domenico di Fiesole, near Firenze in a villa called Riposo dei Vescovi. His wife Anna came from a wealthy family. He traveled a lot to the far East, especially to Indonesia, to the island of Bali. She supported a big part of his projects. He died on 23 April 1950 in San Domenico di Fiesole in Italy.
